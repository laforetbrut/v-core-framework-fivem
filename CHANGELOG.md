# Changelog

All notable changes to FiveM Vanilla Dev Server are documented here.

---

## [1.0.0] - 2026-07-08

### Added (English first)

- **Player frisk / steal + hands-up (v-inventory, RP)** — press the surrender key (**X**, rebindable) to raise your hands (synced state + anim). Another player can then press the **search** key (**H**) near you to open your **searchable inventory** as a container and **take or plant** items. Server-authoritative: proximity is validated on open **and on every move** (no looting from afar), the target must be **hands-up, downed, or you're police/admin**, and the **hidden pocket is never exposed** (only the main inventory is). Taken items sync live to both players.
- **Shared / gang stashes with permissions (v-inventory)** — persistent containers gated by **job (+ min grade), gang, or permission tier**, verified server-side on every open. Open one from a target zone / job menu (`v-inventory:server:openSharedStash`) or another resource (`exports['v-inventory']:OpenSharedStash(src, id)`); configured in `Config.SharedStashes`.
- **Weapons are now functional items (v-inventory)** — using a weapon item **draws / holsters** it (toggle, never consumed); a **serial** is minted on first draw and shown in the tooltip; **ammo boxes** top up the drawn weapon (`Config.AmmoPerItem` rounds each); the weapon's live ammo is **persisted to its metadata** on holster and periodically, so it survives relogs. Moving / dropping / giving the drawn weapon holsters it first. Item names map directly to GTA weapon hashes.
- **Backpacks & body armor (v-inventory)** — carrying a **backpack** item raises the personal capacity by **+12 slots / +20 kg** (`Config.Backpack`); using a **body-armor** item applies armour (`Config.ArmorAmount`). Capacity is now computed per-player (`maxWeightFor` / `maxSlotsFor`) everywhere, so the weight gauge and space checks react to the backpack.
- **Multi-character selection** — on connect the player now gets a **character selection screen** with one slot per permission tier: **user 1, mod 2, admin/superadmin 6** (`Config.CharacterSlots`). Each filled slot shows the name + cash/bank and a **Play** button; empty slots offer **New character** (creation writes into that slot). **admin+** can **delete** one of their own characters (two-click confirm, server permission-gated, removes the character + its bank history). v-core loads all characters per license, validates the slot on create, and gates delete; v-spawn hosts the Field Case selection screen and routes select/create/delete.
- **Appearance suite Phase 2 — barber, plastic surgery, tattoos (`v-appearance`)** — a shared re-openable editor, opened from **peds/blips** at barbershops, a plastic surgeon, and tattoo parlours (E to interact), with a live orbit-camera preview. **Barber:** hairstyle, hair colour + highlight, and every head overlay (eyebrows, beard, chest hair, makeup, blush, lipstick, ageing, complexion, moles, blemishes) with a **style stepper, a real opacity slider and a colour palette** — this fixes the long-standing dead controls (blush had no backing overlay; makeup/lipstick were pinned to opacity 0 and stayed invisible). **Surgeon:** all 20 face features plus head-blend face-shape / skin-tone mixes. **Tattoos:** an **869-overlay catalogue** (real GTA V ped-decoration overlays, grouped by body zone, gender-resolved) — apply/remove per zone, "remove all". Tattoos are stored on `appearance.tattoos` and re-applied by the engine **after** the head-blend (which wipes decorations). Field Case NUI; fr + en.
- **Appearance engine `v-appearance` (Phase 1)** — a new module is now the single writer of the player's ped. Clothing is stored as a **stable (collection, local index, texture) reference** via the ped-collection natives, so adding addon/replace clothing or changing the game build no longer corrupts existing characters (a raw global drawable id does). Existing characters migrate v1→v2 on load; `v-spawn` and `v-clothing` delegate all ped rendering to the engine. Also fixed a latent v-core bug: `appearance` was missing from the autosave `UPDATE`, so an equip just before a crash was lost.
- **Hidden pocket (v-inventory)** — a **1 kg concealed compartment** on the player, shown as a locked panel ("Not visible on a search"). It is stored in the character's **metadata**, separate from the main inventory, so a future police frisk/steal — which reads the new `GetSearchable` export (main inventory only) — can never reveal it. Drag items in and out like any container.
- **Shop inventory panel + drag-to-buy (v-shops)** — the store now shows **your inventory** beside the catalogue and lets you **drag a catalogue item onto it to buy** (1 unit; the Buy button + stepper remain for quantities). Catalogue and inventory tiles show item images (with the same garment/box fallback as the inventory).

### Performance (English first)

- **Inventory payloads slimmed** — the item catalogue (`defs`, ~170 rows) is now sent to the NUI **once on open** and cached client-side; every move / use / drop / give response omits it, so per-action payloads shrank from the full catalogue to just the changed state. **Ground drops** became real world props that are **garbage-collected the moment they're emptied** — fixing the previously unbounded `Stashes` growth (drops were never removed, even when empty). Drop position is also now taken from the player's server-side coords (not a client-supplied point).

### Changed (English first)

- **"Field Case" rolled out to every module** — the inventory's art direction is now the project-wide design language. `v-ui/theme.css` was rewritten as its canonical source: warm-charcoal surfaces, the orange-only accent rule, muted status/rarity hues, a `.v-chamfer` primitive (1px border layer + fill layer, both `clip-path`'d, drop-shadow on the cheap solid layer so hover never repaints the children), `.v-tab` stencil parallelograms, `.v-brk` L-brackets, `.v-slot` foam cutouts, `.v-gauge` notch strips, a `--z-*` scale, a global `:focus-visible` ring and a `prefers-reduced-motion` block. **v-hud** (restrained overlay + fully chamfered F7 panel, hard-notch toggles, square slider thumbs), **v-notify** (chamfered toasts with a type-keyed rule and machined brackets), **v-loadscreen** (blueprint grid + film grain over the footage, stencil title, 16-notch progress gauge), **v-spawn** (chamfered editor dock, recessed sliders, spawn-point cards as machined nameplates), **v-clothing**, **v-shops**, **v-banking** (the balance as a Consolas hero figure) and **v-admin** (gauge-cluster dashboard tiles, stencil log tags, orange rail rule) all migrated. The rule is now written into `RULES.md` §3.5 as mandatory for any new NUI page.
- **Design-system bugs found during the rollout** — `.okflash` / `.badflash` in v-admin painted a **permanent** success/error ring instead of a transient flash (the `void el.offsetWidth` reflow in `flash()` shows an animation was always intended); they now decay over 900 ms. The v-admin time slider and freeze checkbox were still native controls (pill track, 13px OS box) and became a recessed well with a square machined thumb and a hard-notch checkbox. v-clothing's category strip scrolled horizontally with a **hidden** scrollbar, silently clipping the last three categories — it now wraps. Free clothing (`price 0`) reads as a **"Free" stencil badge** instead of `$0`. v-shops' item names were truncated at any realistic length (panel widened to 530px) and its category rings still used the old bright palette. `#E86A5E` had been copy-pasted as a literal into six files and is now the `--v-danger-300` token.
- **Inventory art direction — "Field Case"** — the whole inventory was redesigned around one object: a ruggedized equipment flight-case cracked open on a workbench. Panels are **chamfered** (top-left / bottom-right cut, drawn as a 1px border layer + a fill layer so nothing gets clipped), each carries an **orange stencil parallelogram tab** riding the top edge and **machined L-brackets** on the sharp corners. Slots became **foam cutouts** (recessed inset shadows instead of raised cards, radii ≤3px) whose rarity now reads as a coloured inner ring — only *legendary* and *mythic* are allowed to bloom, every other hue was desaturated so **orange stays the only saturated colour on screen**. The weight bar is a **recessed 16-notch gauge** that shifts to red past 90%, paired with a right-aligned **Consolas caliper readout** (`18.73 / 30.00 KG`). Typography splits into Bahnschrift Condensed for tracked uppercase labels and Consolas for tight tabular numbers. The backdrop layers a blueprint micro-grid, an SVG film-grain tile and a hard vignette. Opening plays one orchestrated sequence: panels seat 70 ms apart, then slots ripple in 12 ms apart (driven by a `--i` custom property).
- **Inventory accessibility** — `--v-text-faint` was raised from `#63616C` (≈2.6:1, failing) to `#8F877E` (≈5.0:1) and every sub-10px string (slot labels, weight badges, equipment names) is now **10px minimum**. Added a global `:focus-visible` ring, a `prefers-reduced-motion` block that kills every entrance/hover animation, `aria-label`s on the search boxes, amount/rename inputs and wallet chip, `aria-hidden` on decorative SVGs, a formal z-index token scale, and micro-interaction transitions lengthened from 60–120 ms to 150–200 ms.

### Fixed (in-game interface pass)

- **Inventory `openStash` exploit (CRITICAL) + fractional amounts** — the trunk/stash open trusted a client-supplied id, so a modded client could open **any** vehicle's trunk by plate from anywhere and even write arbitrary rows into the `stashes` table. The client now sends the vehicle's **net id**; the server resolves the entity, **checks the player is within 5 m**, and derives the stash id from the **server-read plate**. Ground drops must reference a real drop. Move / drop / give amounts are now **floored** (no fractional stacks), and the stash `kind` is finally sent to the NUI.
- **Drag-drop stacking / placement (inventory + shops)** — dragging a stackable item onto a matching one did not merge, and drops (incl. drag-to-buy in shops) did not land on the targeted slot. Two root causes: oxmysql returns `TINYINT(1)` as a **boolean**, so `stackable == 1` / `usable == 1` silently failed (now coerced to 0/1 on load); and the merge condition tested `not metadata`, but an **empty table `{}` is truthy in Lua**, blocking every merge (now a `noMeta` helper treats nil and `{}` alike). `addToContainer` / `AddItem` now accept a **preferred slot**, so an inventory move and a shop drag-to-buy land where you dropped them (stacking onto a match there, else placing in the empty cell).
- **Spawn fell into the void / showed a default ped** — the default `spawnmanager` revealed a nude freemode ped at the fallback point (often above the ocean) before v-spawn took over, then the GTA switch swooped onto ground that had not streamed yet, so the player fell. The screen is now held **black from the first frame** until a flow takes control; in `switchSpawn` the ped is kept **frozen** at the destination while collision streams and a solid ground Z is found (retried), and is unfrozen **only after** the switch-in completes. The character is also dressed while the screen is black.
- **Inventory drag & drop did nothing in-game** — it used **HTML5 drag-and-drop**, which FiveM's CEF 103 does not fire reliably (it worked in a normal browser, not in-game). Rewritten with **pointer events** (mousedown → a ghost that follows the cursor → mouseup), the technique every mainstream FiveM inventory uses. A plain click no longer triggers a move; equipping by dragging a clothing item onto a body slot still works.
- **Items with no image** — clothing items are inserted with no raster image, so they showed blank. Slots now render a **fallback icon**: a garment glyph for clothing, a generic box for anything else.
- **No mouse cursor in any menu (inventory, admin, banking, shops, clothing)** — the panels rendered but the player could not interact with them. The shared focus helper called `SetNuiFocus` from inside **v-core**, and that native is **scoped to the calling resource**: it returns early when the caller has no NUI frame, and otherwise focuses the caller's own page. `v-core` declares no `ui_page`, so the call was a **silent no-op** — no error, no log line. v-hud (F7) and v-spawn were unaffected because they still called the native locally, which made the bug look module-specific. `v-core/client/focus.lua` now keeps only the reference-counted `nuiOpen` bookkeeping (`MenuOpened` / `MenuClosed` / `IsAnyMenuOpen`), and each owning resource takes and releases focus itself.
- **Premium inventory restyle** — a Quasar-grade visual polish (keeping the orange identity): layered panel gradients + top accent glow + depth shadows, glossier segmented weight bar, framed quick-slots column, richer slots (inner highlight, hover lift + glow, crisper rarity glows, fixed item-image sizing), refined tooltip/context/amount cards and micro-animations. Also: **arms/gloves are now free** in the clothing store.
- **Custom square minimap (v-hud)** — the radar mask is swapped for a square texture (QBCore method, shipped `squaremap.ytd`): the map becomes a clean square and the GTA:O green/blue health & armour bars are **no longer drawn at all**. It's fully **repositionable** — drag it anywhere in layout mode (the player's frame offset shifts the map), no decorative frame in normal mode, aspect-ratio corrected, per-player persistence, and the vanilla mask is restored on resource stop. Hunger & thirst rings are now **always readable** (never fade) and a **one-shot alert fires at 25%** hunger / thirst (amber ring pulse + notification, re-armed above 32%).
- **Clothing-scan kick** — the thumbnail scan kicked the admin (reliable net-event overflow: each screenshot was sent as a ~200-500 KB base64 blob over `TriggerServerEvent`). Captures now go through an HTTP pipeline: the NUI downscales each shot to a 384px square jpeg (~4-40 KB) and POSTs it to the resource's `SetHttpHandler` endpoint, authenticated by a one-shot scan token; the scan also aborts cleanly after 8 consecutive upload failures instead of looping.

- **CEF black-box bug** — removed every `backdrop-filter: blur()` (renders as opaque black on FiveM's Chromium 103 CEF) across v-hud, v-spawn, v-banking, v-notify.
- **Compass box + clicks** — explicit compass width + `overflow:hidden` (kills the oversized black box) and `pointer-events:none` on decorative HUD widgets so they no longer swallow the character-creator's clicks.
- **color-mix()** — replaced (unsupported before Chromium 111) with static tints in v-notify.
- **Creation instance** — new players isolated in a private routing bucket so simultaneous joins don't collide.
- **Loadscreen video** — fixed the video not showing (negative z-index painted it behind the body background); switched to a lighter H.264 MP4 that reliably plays in CEF.
- **Robustness** — v-banking releases NUI focus on resource stop; `dependency 'v-ui'` added; creation flow guards natives so the screen always fades back in; default GTA cash HUD hidden.
- **Animations** — critical-vital pulse + compass drop-in.

### Performance (miroir français)

- **Charges NUI de l'inventaire allégées** — le catalogue d'items (`defs`, ~170 lignes) n'est envoyé à la NUI **qu'à l'ouverture** puis mis en cache ; chaque réponse de déplacement / usage / jet / don l'omet, donc la charge par action passe du catalogue complet au seul état modifié. Les **drops au sol** deviennent de vrais objets du monde **nettoyés dès qu'ils sont vidés** — corrige la croissance non bornée de `Stashes` (les drops n'étaient jamais supprimés). La position du drop vient aussi des coords serveur du joueur (plus d'un point fourni par le client).

Les noms d'items de v-shops étaient tronqués à toute longueur réaliste (panneau élargi à 530px) et ses pastilles de catégorie utilisaient encore l'ancienne palette vive. `#E86A5E` avait été copié-collé en littéral dans six fichiers et devient le token `--v-danger-300`.
- **Direction artistique de l'inventaire — « Field Case »** — l'inventaire entier a été redessiné autour d'un seul objet : une mallette d'équipement blindée ouverte sur un établi. Les panneaux sont **chanfreinés** (coins haut-gauche / bas-droit coupés, dessinés via une couche bordure 1px + une couche de remplissage pour ne rien rogner), chacun porte un **onglet pochoir orange en parallélogramme** à cheval sur le bord supérieur et des **équerres usinées** aux angles vifs. Les slots sont devenus des **découpes de mousse** (ombres internes creusées au lieu de cartes en relief, rayons ≤3px) dont la rareté se lit comme un anneau intérieur coloré — seuls *légendaire* et *mythique* ont droit à la lueur, toutes les autres teintes ont été désaturées pour que **l'orange reste la seule couleur saturée à l'écran**. La barre de poids est une **jauge encastrée à 16 crans** qui bascule au rouge au-delà de 90 %, accompagnée d'un **relevé au pied à coulisse en Consolas** aligné à droite (`18.73 / 30.00 KG`). La typographie se scinde : Bahnschrift Condensed pour les libellés capitales espacées, Consolas pour les chiffres tabulaires serrés. Le fond superpose une micro-grille de plan, une texture de grain SVG et un vignettage franc. L'ouverture joue une séquence orchestrée : les panneaux se posent à 70 ms d'intervalle, puis les slots se remplissent en cascade à 12 ms d'intervalle (piloté par une propriété custom `--i`).
- **Accessibilité de l'inventaire** — `--v-text-faint` est passé de `#63616C` (≈2,6:1, non conforme) à `#8F877E` (≈5,0:1) et tout texte sous 10px (libellés de slot, badges de poids, noms d'équipement) est désormais à **10px minimum**. Ajout d'un anneau `:focus-visible` global, d'un bloc `prefers-reduced-motion` qui coupe toutes les animations d'entrée et de survol, d'`aria-label` sur les champs de recherche, les champs quantité/renommage et la pastille d'argent, d'`aria-hidden` sur les SVG décoratifs, d'une échelle formelle de tokens z-index, et des transitions de micro-interaction rallongées de 60–120 ms à 150–200 ms.

### Correctifs (miroir français)

- **Faille `openStash` de l'inventaire (CRITIQUE) + montants fractionnaires** — l'ouverture de coffre faisait confiance à un id fourni par le client : un client modifié pouvait ouvrir **n'importe quel** coffre par sa plaque depuis n'importe où et même écrire des lignes arbitraires dans `stashes`. Le client envoie désormais le **net id** du véhicule ; le serveur résout l'entité, **vérifie que le joueur est à moins de 5 m** et déduit l'id de la **plaque lue côté serveur**. Les drops au sol doivent référencer un vrai drop. Les montants de déplacement / jet / don sont **arrondis à l'entier** (plus de piles fractionnaires), et le `kind` du stash est enfin envoyé à la NUI.
- **Le spawn tombait dans le vide / montrait un ped par défaut** — le `spawnmanager` par défaut révélait un ped freemode nu au point de repli (souvent au-dessus de l'océan) avant que v-spawn ne prenne la main, puis le switch GTA plongeait sur un sol non encore streamé, d'où la chute. L'écran est désormais tenu **noir dès la première frame** jusqu'à ce qu'un flux prenne le contrôle ; dans `switchSpawn` le ped reste **gelé** au point cible pendant que la collision se stream et qu'un sol solide est trouvé (avec réessais), et n'est dégelé **qu'après** la fin du switch-in. Le personnage est aussi habillé pendant que l'écran est noir.
- **Le glisser-déposer de l'inventaire ne faisait rien en jeu** — il utilisait le **drag-and-drop HTML5**, que le CEF 103 de FiveM ne déclenche pas de façon fiable (ça marchait dans un vrai navigateur, pas en jeu). Réécrit en **événements souris** (mousedown → un fantôme qui suit le curseur → mouseup), la technique de tous les inventaires FiveM courants. Un simple clic ne déclenche plus de déplacement ; équiper un vêtement par glisser sur un slot du corps fonctionne toujours.
- **Items sans image** — les vêtements sont insérés sans image raster, ils s'affichaient donc vides. Les slots rendent maintenant un **icône de repli** : une silhouette de vêtement pour les habits, un colis générique sinon.
- **Aucun curseur de souris dans les menus (inventaire, admin, banque, boutiques, vêtements)** — les panneaux s'affichaient mais le joueur ne pouvait pas interagir. Le gestionnaire de focus partagé appelait `SetNuiFocus` depuis **v-core**, or ce native est **relatif à la ressource appelante** : il sort immédiatement si l'appelant n'a pas de frame NUI, et sinon il donne le focus à sa propre page. `v-core` ne déclare aucun `ui_page`, donc l'appel ne faisait **strictement rien, en silence** — aucune erreur, aucune ligne de log. v-hud (F7) et v-spawn n'étaient pas touchés car ils appelaient encore le native localement, ce qui faisait passer le bug pour un problème propre à chaque module. `v-core/client/focus.lua` ne conserve plus que la comptabilité à compteur de références de `nuiOpen` (`MenuOpened` / `MenuClosed` / `IsAnyMenuOpen`), et chaque ressource propriétaire prend et relâche le focus elle-même.
- **Minimap carrée custom (v-hud)** — le masque du radar est remplacé par une texture carrée (méthode QBCore, `squaremap.ytd` embarqué) : la map devient un carré propre et les barres vie/armure vert/bleu de GTA:O **ne sont plus dessinées du tout**. Entièrement **déplaçable** — glisse-la où tu veux en mode disposition (l'offset du cadre décale la map), aucun cadre décoratif en mode normal, correction du ratio d'écran, persistance par joueur, et le masque d'origine est restauré à l'arrêt de la ressource. Les jauges de faim & soif restent **toujours lisibles** (ne s'estompent jamais) et une **alerte à 25 %** de faim / soif se déclenche (pulsation ambre + notification, ré-armée au-dessus de 32 %).
- **Kick pendant le scan de vêtements** — le scan de vignettes kickait l'admin (dépassement des events réseau fiables : chaque screenshot partait en blob base64 de ~200-500 Ko via `TriggerServerEvent`). Les captures passent désormais par un pipeline HTTP : le NUI réduit chaque photo en jpeg carré 384px (~4-40 Ko) et la POST vers l'endpoint `SetHttpHandler` de la ressource, authentifié par un jeton de scan à usage unique ; le scan s'interrompt aussi proprement après 8 échecs d'envoi consécutifs au lieu de boucler.
